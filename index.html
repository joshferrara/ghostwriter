The problem

Generic AI has a tell. You don't sound like that.

"I hope this email finds you well." Forced enthusiasm. That smooth, sourceless cadence. You can spot it in a second, and so can the people reading your email. Same message, two different tones:

Generic AI

"I hope this email finds you well! I wanted to circle back regarding the contract. Please let me know at your earliest convenience. Thanks so much!"

You, via Ghostwriter

"hey, quick question. did the contract ever get signed? no rush, just checking some things off my list."

How it works

Point it at your writing. It picks up the pattern.

No prompt engineering project. No settings panel. Just a short conversation with your agent.

1

Point it at your sent messages

Email, texts, Slack, exports. Whatever you already have. It reads only what you confirm, and only what you sent.

2

It writes your style guide

It turns your habits into one portable ghostwriter-guide.md: openings, tone, brevity, and the way you make an ask.

3

Your agent drafts as you

From then on, the guide is the authority. Drafts start in your voice, then a light cleanup removes AI slop without sanding off the parts that sound like you.

The Generate task builds your style guide. The Use task applies it when your agent drafts or edits for you.

What you get

Your voice, written down.

One readable markdown file that captures how you write, ready to paste into any agent that needs to draft in your voice.

# Alex's Writing Style Guide

## Openings
- "Hey [Name]!" Warm, the default.
- "Hi all," For broader or formal threads.

## Tone mechanics
- Exclamation points: frequent, one at a time
- Emoji: 🙏 😅 in chat; never in client email

## What to avoid
- Don't open with "I hope this finds you well."
- Never stiff, salesy, or buzzword-heavy.
